# Sprint 1 - Data Acquisition, Cleaning and Exploration

## Week 1-3

1. Set up project environment, GitHub repository, and documentations.

2. Define your hypothesis based on a business question, such as: What factors influence the annual salary of developers in the community? For example:

* Demographics data significantly influence salary levels
* Technology and experience are crucial drivers
* Used tools, experience, and locations are the main factors
* Experience, education, position, and industry are the main factors
* Your own hypothesis for investigation

3. Define a set of 10-15 questions based on your chosen hypothesis to gain insights from the data. 

* Is there a correlation between ‘Years of professional work experience’ and ‘Total annual compensation’?
* What is the effect of the highest level of formal education on starting salaries for those with less than two years of experience? 
* Does a Master’s degree offer a substantial salary increase compared to a Bachelor’s degree?
* How does the number of years of professional experience vary across different age groups?
* What are the most popular combinations of Cloud Platforms and Databases? (e.g., Are AWS users more likely to use PostgreSQL?)
* Are developers sceptical about vibe coding, and is there a difference between junior and senior developers?
* What are the main ways professional developers learn AI?
* Does the type of work environment (remote, in-person, hybrid) have a significant impact on salary?
* How many unique software applications do professional developers and students typically use? 
* Can you ask more questions to get interesting information from the data?

_Note_: The questions above are just an example and do not support any hypothesis. More could be found [here](https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2025/).

Questions form the basis of your investigation and help you choose your further steps in data cleaning. They enable you to define a subset of variables of interest and clean only the data necessary to answer the questions in the context of your hypothesis.

4. Raw data requires extensive preprocessing to ensure analytical integrity and includes but not limited to the following step:
* Import necessary datasets
* Validate consistency and data quality 
* Remove duplicate entries and entries with missing values for the dependent variable.
* Explore the outliers
* Reorganise categorical variables with many categories
* Any other steps you find necessary to complete  



### Data products
* Hypothesis, research questions, key insights and visualisations (demographics, location, top technologies, etc.)
* All data manipulations are scripted for reproducibility and uploaded to GitHub Repository
* Documentation includes a data dictionary, reports, notebooks and pitch-presentation (slides in .pdf for 5-minute presentation of results achieved in Sprint 1)
